# Common Isolation Exercises

## What Are Isolation Exercises?
Isolation exercises target a single muscle group through a single-joint movement. They complement compound lifts by addressing weak points, muscle imbalances, and hypertrophy goals.

## Upper Body Isolation

### Bicep Curl
- **Target**: Biceps brachii
- **Variations**: Barbell curl, dumbbell curl, hammer curl, preacher curl, incline curl
- **Tips**: Control the eccentric. Avoid swinging. Full range of motion.
- **Sets/Reps**: 3-4x10-15

### Tricep Extension
- **Target**: Triceps brachii (all three heads)
- **Variations**: Overhead extension, skull crusher, cable pushdown, dips
- **Tips**: Keep elbows stationary. Focus on lockout.
- **Sets/Reps**: 3-4x10-15

### Lateral Raise
- **Target**: Lateral (medial) deltoid
- **Variations**: Dumbbell, cable, machine
- **Tips**: Lead with elbows, not hands. Slight forward lean. Light weight, high reps.
- **Sets/Reps**: 3-4x12-20

### Face Pull
- **Target**: Rear deltoids, external rotators, middle traps
- **Why important**: Essential for shoulder health and posture, especially for people who bench press frequently
- **Sets/Reps**: 3-4x15-20

## Lower Body Isolation

### Leg Extension
- **Target**: Quadriceps
- **Tips**: Control the movement. Don't lock out aggressively. Good for quad pump after squats.

### Leg Curl
- **Target**: Hamstrings
- **Variations**: Lying, seated, standing
- **Tips**: Important for knee health and hamstring/quad balance.

### Calf Raise
- **Target**: Gastrocnemius (standing), soleus (seated)
- **Tips**: Full stretch at bottom, pause at top. Calves respond to high volume: 4x12-20.

## Programming Principle
Isolation exercises should come AFTER compound lifts in a workout. They are accessories, not the main course. Typically 2-4 isolation exercises per session.
