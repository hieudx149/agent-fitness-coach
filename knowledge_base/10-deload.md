# Deload Week

## What Is a Deload?
A deload is a planned period of reduced training stress (typically 1 week) to allow the body to recover, adapt, and prepare for the next training block. It is NOT a week off — you still train, but at reduced intensity and/or volume.

## Why Deload?
- Accumulated fatigue from weeks of hard training reduces performance
- Joints, tendons, and ligaments recover slower than muscles
- Mental freshness — prevents burnout and keeps motivation high
- Allows supercompensation: the body "catches up" and you come back stronger

## When to Deload
- **Proactive**: Every 4-6 weeks as part of a periodized program
- **Reactive**: When you notice signs of overreaching:
  - Strength plateau or regression for 2+ sessions
  - Persistent joint pain or soreness
  - Poor sleep quality
  - Decreased motivation to train
  - Elevated resting heart rate

## How to Deload

### Option 1: Reduce Volume (Recommended)
Keep intensity (weight) the same, cut sets by 40-50%.
- Normal: 4x8 at 80kg → Deload: 2x8 at 80kg

### Option 2: Reduce Intensity
Keep sets/reps the same, reduce weight by 40-50%.
- Normal: 4x5 at 100kg → Deload: 4x5 at 60kg

### Option 3: Reduce Both
Cut volume and intensity moderately.
- Normal: 4x8 at 80kg → Deload: 2x8 at 60kg

## Common Mistake
Skipping deloads because you feel "fine." Fatigue is cumulative and often not felt until it's too late. Plan deloads proactively. You will come back stronger.
