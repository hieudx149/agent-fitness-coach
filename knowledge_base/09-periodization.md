# Periodization

## Definition
Periodization is the systematic planning of training into distinct phases (periods) to optimize performance, prevent overtraining, and peak at the right time. It organizes training variables (volume, intensity, exercise selection) across weeks and months.

## Training Blocks

### Macrocycle
The overall training plan, typically 6-12 months or a full competitive season.

### Mesocycle
A training block within the macrocycle, typically 3-6 weeks with a specific focus (hypertrophy, strength, peaking).

### Microcycle
Usually one training week. The smallest planning unit.

## Common Periodization Models

### Linear Periodization
Gradually increase intensity while decreasing volume over time.
- Phase 1 (Hypertrophy): 4x10-12 at 65-70% 1RM (4 weeks)
- Phase 2 (Strength): 4x6-8 at 75-80% 1RM (4 weeks)
- Phase 3 (Power/Peak): 3x3-5 at 85-90% 1RM (3 weeks)
- Phase 4 (Deload): 2x5 at 60% 1RM (1 week)

Best for: Beginners and early intermediates.

### Undulating Periodization (DUP)
Vary intensity and volume within each week.
- Monday: Heavy (4x4 at 85%)
- Wednesday: Moderate (4x8 at 72%)
- Friday: Light/Speed (3x6 at 65% with focus on bar speed)

Best for: Intermediate to advanced lifters. Provides varied stimulus and prevents staleness.

### Block Periodization
Concentrate on one quality per mesocycle.
- Block 1: Accumulation (high volume, moderate intensity)
- Block 2: Transmutation (moderate volume, high intensity)
- Block 3: Realization (low volume, peak intensity)

Best for: Advanced athletes preparing for competition.

## Key Principle
No single periodization model is "best." The right model depends on training age, goals, schedule, and individual response. The most important thing is having a structured plan rather than training randomly.
