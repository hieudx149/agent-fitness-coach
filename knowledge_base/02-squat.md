# Barbell Back Squat

## Overview
The barbell back squat is a fundamental compound movement targeting the entire lower body. It is considered the king of all exercises for building leg strength and overall athleticism.

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Quadriceps, glutes, adductors
- **Secondary**: Hamstrings, erector spinae, core
- **Stabilizers**: Calves, hip flexors

## Proper Form
1. Position the bar on your upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar)
2. Unrack and step back with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out
3. Initiate by pushing hips back and bending knees simultaneously
4. Descend until hip crease is below the top of the knee (parallel or below)
5. Drive through the full foot to stand back up
6. Keep chest up and maintain a neutral spine throughout

## High Bar vs Low Bar
- **High bar**: Bar sits on upper traps. More upright torso. Greater quad emphasis. Preferred by Olympic lifters.
- **Low bar**: Bar sits on rear delts. More forward lean. Greater posterior chain emphasis. Preferred by powerlifters.

## Common Mistakes
- Knees caving inward (valgus collapse)
- Excessive forward lean / "good morning" squat
- Rising on toes / heels lifting
- Not reaching adequate depth
- Butt wink at the bottom (excessive lumbar flexion)

## Programming Recommendations
- **Beginners**: 3x8-10 at RPE 6-7, twice per week
- **Intermediate**: 4x5-8 at RPE 7-8, with front squat or leg press as accessory
- **Advanced**: Periodized with heavy triples, pause squats, tempo variations

## Safety
- Use a squat rack with safety pins set at appropriate height
- Learn to bail safely (dump the bar behind you)
- Wear flat-soled shoes or squat shoes for stability
