# Muscle Recovery

## The Recovery Process
Muscles don't grow in the gym — they grow during recovery. Training creates micro-damage to muscle fibers. Recovery is when the body repairs and strengthens those fibers (supercompensation).

## Recovery Timeline
- **0-24 hours**: Inflammation and muscle soreness begin (DOMS)
- **24-48 hours**: Peak soreness for most people
- **48-72 hours**: Most muscle groups are recovered enough to train again
- **72-96 hours**: Full recovery for heavy compound lifts

## Factors Affecting Recovery

### Sleep (Most Important)
- Aim for 7-9 hours per night
- Growth hormone is released primarily during deep sleep
- Poor sleep = poor recovery = poor gains
- Consistent sleep schedule matters more than total hours

### Nutrition
- **Protein**: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight per day for muscle building
- **Calories**: Must be in a caloric surplus to build muscle optimally; maintenance calories to maintain
- **Timing**: Spread protein intake across 3-5 meals. Post-workout nutrition is helpful but not as critical as total daily intake.

### Hydration
- Dehydration impairs performance and recovery
- Aim for 2-3 liters of water per day, more if training intensely or in hot environments

### Stress Management
- Chronic psychological stress elevates cortisol, which impairs recovery
- Training is a stressor — manage total life stress, not just gym stress

## Active Recovery
Light activity on rest days can improve recovery:
- Walking (20-30 minutes)
- Light stretching or yoga
- Swimming
- Foam rolling

## Signs of Insufficient Recovery
- Persistent soreness lasting more than 72 hours
- Strength regression over multiple sessions
- Increased injury rate
- Poor sleep quality
- Loss of motivation
