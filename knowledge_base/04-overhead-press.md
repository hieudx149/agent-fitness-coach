# Overhead Press (OHP)

## Overview
The standing barbell overhead press is a compound movement that builds shoulder strength and stability. It requires full-body tension and is one of the best indicators of upper-body pressing strength.

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Anterior and lateral deltoids
- **Secondary**: Triceps, upper chest (clavicular pec), traps
- **Stabilizers**: Core, glutes, erector spinae

## Proper Form
1. Unrack the bar at collarbone height with a grip slightly wider than shoulder width
2. Stand with feet hip-width apart, glutes and core braced
3. Press the bar straight up, moving your head back slightly to clear the chin
4. Once the bar passes your forehead, push your head forward ("through the window")
5. Lock out overhead with the bar directly over mid-foot
6. Lower under control back to the starting position

## Common Mistakes
- Excessive lower back arch (lack of core bracing)
- Pressing the bar forward instead of straight up
- Not locking out fully overhead
- Using leg drive (turning it into a push press unintentionally)

## Programming Recommendations
- **Beginners**: 3x8-10, twice per week
- **Intermediate**: 4x5-8, with dumbbell press or push press as variation
- **Advanced**: Heavy singles/doubles with back-off volume work
