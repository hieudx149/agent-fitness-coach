# Bench Press

## Overview
The barbell bench press is a compound upper-body exercise that primarily targets the pectoralis major (chest), anterior deltoids (front shoulders), and triceps. It is one of the three competition lifts in powerlifting.

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Pectoralis major (sternal and clavicular heads)
- **Secondary**: Anterior deltoid, triceps brachii
- **Stabilizers**: Rotator cuff, serratus anterior, core

## Proper Form
1. Lie flat on a bench with feet firmly on the floor
2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width
3. Unrack the bar and position it over your chest with arms extended
4. Lower the bar in a controlled manner to mid-chest (nipple line)
5. Touch the chest lightly — do not bounce
6. Press the bar back up to the starting position
7. Keep shoulder blades retracted and depressed throughout

## Common Mistakes
- Flaring elbows to 90 degrees (increases shoulder injury risk; aim for 45-75 degrees)
- Lifting hips off the bench
- Bouncing the bar off the chest
- Uneven grip width
- Not using a spotter for heavy sets

## Programming Recommendations
- **Beginners**: 3x8-12 at RPE 6-7, twice per week
- **Intermediate**: 4x6-8 at RPE 7-8, with variation (incline, close-grip) on second day
- **Advanced**: Periodized approach with heavy singles/doubles and volume blocks

## Safety
- Always use a spotter for sets near failure
- Use safety pins/arms in a power rack when training alone
- Learn the "roll of shame" as a backup escape technique
