# Warm-Up and Cool-Down

## Why Warm Up?
- Increases blood flow to muscles and joints
- Raises core body temperature for better muscle elasticity
- Activates the nervous system for stronger contractions
- Reduces injury risk
- Improves performance (you will lift more after warming up)

## Warm-Up Protocol

### Phase 1: General Warm-Up (5 minutes)
Light cardio to raise heart rate:
- Walking or light jogging
- Rowing machine
- Cycling
- Jump rope

### Phase 2: Dynamic Stretching (5 minutes)
Movement-based stretches for the muscles you'll use:
- Leg swings (front-back and side-to-side)
- Arm circles
- Hip circles
- Bodyweight squats
- Inchworms
- World's greatest stretch

### Phase 3: Specific Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)
Ramp-up sets of your first exercise:
- Set 1: Empty bar × 10
- Set 2: 50% working weight × 8
- Set 3: 70% working weight × 5
- Set 4: 85% working weight × 3
- Set 5: 90% working weight × 1-2
- Then begin working sets

## Cool-Down

### What to Do
- 5 minutes of light cardio (walking, easy cycling)
- Static stretching for trained muscle groups (hold each 20-30 seconds)
- Foam rolling for tight areas (optional but helpful)

### What NOT to Do
- Don't do intense static stretching BEFORE training (can reduce force production)
- Don't skip warm-up even if "short on time" — cut a set from your workout instead

## Key Point
A proper warm-up takes 10-15 minutes. It is the cheapest insurance against injury. No one ever regretted warming up.
