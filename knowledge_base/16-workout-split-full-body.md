# Full Body Training

## Overview
Full body training involves training all major muscle groups in every session. It is the oldest and most time-tested approach to resistance training.

## Schedule
- Monday: Full Body
- Wednesday: Full Body
- Friday: Full Body

3 sessions per week, each muscle group trained **3x per week**.

## Sample Full Body Workout A
1. Squat — 3x5
2. Bench Press — 3x8
3. Barbell Row — 3x8
4. Overhead Press — 2x10
5. Bicep Curl — 2x12
6. Calf Raise — 2x15

## Sample Full Body Workout B
1. Deadlift — 3x5
2. Incline Dumbbell Press — 3x10
3. Pull-Ups — 3x8
4. Leg Press — 3x10
5. Face Pull — 3x15
6. Tricep Pushdown — 2x12

Alternate A and B each session.

## Advantages
- Highest training frequency (3x per week per muscle)
- Most time-efficient — only 3 days in the gym
- Excellent for beginners (practice movements often)
- Great for fat loss (high metabolic demand per session)
- Research suggests higher frequency may be superior for hypertrophy at equal volume

## Disadvantages
- Lower volume per muscle per session
- Workouts can feel long if you add too many exercises
- Fatigue management is important — exercise order matters
- Less time for isolation work

## Who Is It For?
- Beginners (best starting split)
- Busy people who can only train 3 days per week
- Older trainees who benefit from frequent movement practice
- Anyone returning to training after a break
