# Barbell Row

## Overview
The barbell bent-over row is a compound pulling exercise that targets the entire back. It is essential for balanced upper-body development and posture improvement.

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, middle/lower traps
- **Secondary**: Rear deltoids, biceps, erector spinae
- **Stabilizers**: Core, hamstrings (isometric)

## Proper Form
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, bar over mid-foot
2. Hinge at the hips until your torso is roughly 45 degrees to the floor
3. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width (overhand or underhand)
4. Pull the bar toward your lower chest / upper abdomen
5. Squeeze shoulder blades together at the top
6. Lower the bar under control

## Variations
- **Pendlay Row**: Bar returns to floor each rep. More explosive. Stricter form.
- **Underhand Row (Yates Row)**: Supinated grip. More bicep involvement. Targets lower lats.
- **Dumbbell Row**: Single-arm. Allows greater range of motion and addresses imbalances.

## Common Mistakes
- Using too much body English (swinging/momentum)
- Not maintaining a flat back
- Pulling to the wrong position (too high or too low)
- Standing too upright (reduces back engagement)

## Programming Recommendations
- **Beginners**: 3x8-12, once or twice per week
- **Intermediate**: 4x6-10, paired with a vertical pull (pull-ups/lat pulldown)
- Row volume should roughly match pressing volume for shoulder health
