# Upper/Lower Split

## Overview
The upper/lower split divides training into upper-body days and lower-body days, typically run 4 days per week. It is one of the most versatile and effective training splits.

## Schedule
- Monday: Upper Body
- Tuesday: Lower Body
- Wednesday: Rest
- Thursday: Upper Body
- Friday: Lower Body
- Saturday/Sunday: Rest

Each muscle group trained **twice per week** with built-in recovery days.

## Sample Upper Body Day (Strength Focus)
1. Bench Press — 4x5
2. Barbell Row — 4x5
3. Overhead Press — 3x8
4. Pull-Ups — 3x8-10
5. Face Pull — 3x15
6. Bicep Curl — 2x12

## Sample Lower Body Day (Strength Focus)
1. Squat — 4x5
2. Romanian Deadlift — 3x8
3. Leg Press — 3x10
4. Leg Curl — 3x12
5. Calf Raise — 3x15
6. Plank — 3x45s

## Advantages
- Balanced frequency (2x per week per muscle)
- Built-in rest days for recovery
- Works well for both strength and hypertrophy goals
- Flexible — easy to adjust if you miss a day (just shift forward)

## Who Is It For?
- Intermediate lifters
- People who can train 4 days per week
- Those who want a balance of volume and recovery
- Natural lifters benefit from the higher frequency vs bro split

## Comparison to PPL
| Factor | Upper/Lower | PPL |
|--------|------------|-----|
| Days/week | 4 | 6 |
| Volume per session | Moderate | High |
| Frequency | 2x/week | 2x/week |
| Recovery time | More | Less |
| Best for | Most people | Advanced/dedicated |
