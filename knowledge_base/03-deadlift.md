# Deadlift

## Overview
The deadlift is a compound exercise where you lift a loaded barbell from the floor to a standing position. It is arguably the most functional strength exercise, as it mimics the everyday movement of picking things up.

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Erector spinae, glutes, hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Quadriceps, lats, traps, forearms/grip
- **Stabilizers**: Core, hip flexors

## Conventional vs Sumo
- **Conventional**: Feet hip-width apart, hands outside knees. Greater lower back and hamstring demand.
- **Sumo**: Wide stance, hands inside knees. More upright torso. Greater hip and adductor demand.

## Proper Form (Conventional)
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar over mid-foot
2. Hinge at hips, grip the bar just outside your shins
3. Drop hips until shins touch the bar, chest up, back flat
4. Take slack out of the bar by engaging lats ("protect your armpits")
5. Drive through the floor, keeping the bar close to your body
6. Lock out by extending hips and knees simultaneously
7. Lower the bar by hinging at the hips first

## Common Mistakes
- Rounding the lower back
- Starting with hips too low (turning it into a squat)
- Bar drifting away from the body
- Jerking the bar off the floor (instead of smooth pull)
- Hyperextending at the top

## Programming Recommendations
- **Beginners**: 3x5 at RPE 6-7, once per week (recovery demanding)
- **Intermediate**: Work up to a heavy set of 3-5, then back-off sets. Add Romanian deadlifts as accessory.
- **Advanced**: Periodized with deficit deadlifts, block pulls, and competition specificity

## Safety
- Never round your lower back under heavy load
- Use mixed grip or hook grip for heavy sets (or straps for training)
- Deadlifts are taxing on the CNS — manage weekly volume carefully
