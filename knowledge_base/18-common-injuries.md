# Common Training Injuries and Prevention

## Shoulder Impingement
- **Cause**: Excessive pressing volume, poor scapular mechanics, too much flat bench with elbows flared
- **Symptoms**: Pain in the front of the shoulder, especially during pressing overhead or behind the neck
- **Prevention**: Include face pulls and external rotations. Keep elbows at 45-75 degrees on bench press. Balance pushing and pulling volume (1:1 ratio minimum).
- **Recovery**: Reduce overhead pressing, increase rowing and rear delt work, see a physiotherapist if persistent

## Lower Back Pain
- **Cause**: Rounding the lower back during deadlifts/squats, weak core, too much volume too fast
- **Symptoms**: Dull ache or sharp pain in the lumbar region
- **Prevention**: Master hip hinge mechanics. Brace your core properly (360-degree bracing). Progress weight gradually. Include core-specific work (planks, dead bugs, Pallof press).
- **Recovery**: Reduce load, focus on pain-free range of motion, movement is generally better than complete rest

## Knee Pain
- **Cause**: Excessive quad-dominant training, poor squat mechanics, sudden increase in training volume
- **Symptoms**: Pain around or behind the kneecap (patellofemoral pain)
- **Prevention**: Balance quad and hamstring work. Include leg curls. Ensure knees track over toes during squats. Warm up properly.
- **Recovery**: Reduce squatting depth temporarily, focus on low-impact movements, strengthen VMO (inner quad)

## Elbow Tendinitis
- **Cause**: Excessive bicep curl volume, heavy barbell curls with poor form, too much pulling without warm-up
- **Types**: Lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow — outer), medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow — inner)
- **Prevention**: Warm up forearms before pulling/curling. Use moderate weights on isolation exercises. Include wrist curls and extensions.
- **Recovery**: Reduce direct arm work, use eccentric-focused exercises for rehab, consider elbow sleeves

## General Injury Prevention Principles
1. **Warm up properly**: 5-10 minutes general cardio + specific warm-up sets
2. **Progress gradually**: Don't jump weight too fast (max 5-10% per week)
3. **Listen to your body**: Sharp pain = stop. Dull ache = assess and adjust
4. **Balance your training**: Don't neglect muscle groups (especially rear delts, hamstrings, rotator cuff)
5. **Deload regularly**: Every 4-6 weeks to allow connective tissue recovery
6. **Sleep and nutrition**: The foundation of injury prevention
