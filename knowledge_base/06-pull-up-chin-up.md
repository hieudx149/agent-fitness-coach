# Pull-Up and Chin-Up

## Overview
Pull-ups and chin-ups are bodyweight compound exercises that build back and arm strength. They are among the best exercises for developing the lats and overall upper-body pulling strength.

## Pull-Up vs Chin-Up
- **Pull-Up**: Overhand (pronated) grip, wider than shoulder width. Greater lat and teres major emphasis.
- **Chin-Up**: Underhand (supinated) grip, shoulder width. Greater bicep involvement and often easier for beginners.

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi, biceps (more in chin-ups)
- **Secondary**: Rhomboids, lower traps, rear deltoids, forearms
- **Stabilizers**: Core, rotator cuff

## Proper Form
1. Hang from the bar with arms fully extended (dead hang)
2. Retract and depress shoulder blades to initiate the pull
3. Pull until chin clears the bar
4. Lower under control to full extension
5. Avoid swinging or kipping

## Progression for Beginners
- Negative pull-ups (jump to top, lower slowly for 3-5 seconds)
- Band-assisted pull-ups
- Lat pulldown machine as a supplementary exercise
- Inverted rows as a regression

## Programming Recommendations
- **Beginners**: Band-assisted or negatives, 3x5-8
- **Intermediate**: Bodyweight 3-4x8-12, add weight via belt when 12 reps is easy
- **Advanced**: Weighted pull-ups 4x5-8, with bodyweight high-rep sets as finisher
