# One Rep Max (1RM)

## Definition
The one rep max (1RM) is the maximum weight you can lift for a single repetition with proper form. It is the gold standard for measuring absolute strength.

## Why Know Your 1RM?
- Programming: Many programs prescribe weights as a percentage of 1RM
- Progress tracking: Comparing 1RM over time shows true strength gains
- Competition: Powerlifting is scored by 1RM in squat, bench, and deadlift

## How to Test 1RM
1. Warm up thoroughly (general + specific warm-up sets)
2. Work up in increments: bar → 50% → 70% → 80% → 90% → attempt
3. Rest 3-5 minutes between heavy attempts
4. Increase by 2.5-5kg per attempt near your max
5. Your 1RM is the heaviest successful lift with good form
6. Limit to 3-4 true max attempts to avoid excessive fatigue

## Estimated 1RM (e1RM)
You can estimate your 1RM from submaximal sets using formulas:

### Epley Formula (most common)
`1RM = weight × (1 + reps/30)`

### Brzycki Formula
`1RM = weight × (36 / (37 - reps))`

### Example
If you bench press 80kg for 8 reps:
- Epley: 80 × (1 + 8/30) = 80 × 1.267 = **101kg**
- Brzycki: 80 × (36 / 29) = 80 × 1.241 = **99kg**

## Accuracy Notes
- Formulas are most accurate for 1-10 reps
- Above 10 reps, estimates become less reliable
- Individual variation exists — some people are better at high reps vs singles
- e1RM is useful for daily training; actual 1RM testing should be done sparingly (every 8-12 weeks)

## Percentage-Based Training
| %1RM | Typical Reps | Training Goal |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| 90-100% | 1-3 | Max strength / peaking |
| 80-89% | 3-6 | Strength |
| 70-79% | 6-10 | Hypertrophy / strength |
| 60-69% | 10-15 | Hypertrophy / endurance |
| 50-59% | 15+ | Endurance / warm-up |
